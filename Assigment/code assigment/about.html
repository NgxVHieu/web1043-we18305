About Ferrari

Ferrari S.p.A. is an Italian luxury sports car manufacturer based in Maranello, Italy. Founded by Enzo Ferrari in 1939 out of Alfa Romeo's race division as Auto Avio Costruzioni, the company built its first car in 1940, and produced its first Ferrari-badged car in 1947.

The company's headquarters are located at the historic factory at Via Abetone Inferiore in Maranello, a town near Modena, Italy. The iconic symbol of the Ferrari brand is the Cavallino Rampante ("prancing horse") logo, which represents speed, power, and elegance.

Ferrari has a long and successful history in motorsports, particularly in Formula One, where it has won 16 Constructors' Championships and 15 Drivers' Championships. The company also produces a range of high-performance sports cars, including the 812 Superfast, the SF90 Stradale, and the Portofino M.
